understanding "99.95%" availability 

 

all US-EAST amazon web services were down for 6 hours this morning (e.g. reddit/foursquare/quora/heroku all run on AWS and were completely down, reddit still has degraded service right now) 

 

HN has a great discussion on hosting availability, Service Level Agreements, what 99.95% uptime really means and why it doesn't actually mean what we think, why some AWS-based services like Netflix didn't go down but many others did. 

 

on amazon's status page, click "more" at the fifth row, "Amazon Elastic Compute Cloud (N. Virginia)" or see my cache[2]
