functional decomposition is the silver bullet 

 

"Composable programs are easier to reason about. We may (confidentally) determine program behaviour by determining the behaviour of sub-programs -> fewer bugs... Composable programs scale indefinitely, by composing more and more sub-programs." 

-- Tony Morris, What Does Functional Programming Mean [1] 

 

the significance: non-composable programs (~=, i think, stateful programs), the number of possible program states multiplies, causing exponentially compounding complexity. composable programs have linearly growing complexity. 

 

let that sink in. if this theory is correct, functional decomposition allows us to reach the theoretical lower-bound in software complexity. this is the silver bullet, folks.
