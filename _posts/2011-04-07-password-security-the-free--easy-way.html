password security the free & easy way! 

TLDR: google docs + chrome bookmark sync + password generator 

 

I mostly use the same password for all my sites, why is that bad? 

 

In December 2010, Gawker.com, a media and gossip company, was hacked. The hacker published a list of all 1.3 million[1] user accounts and passwords. Here's the first few entries on the list[2]: 

 

jesse@gawker.com jesseo jesseo1 

choire@gawker.com Choire2 arthur 

peter@gawker.com peti almafa 

brett@gawker.com nunzilla boeing 

richardl@gawker.com rlawson bambola 

michael@gawker.com Lodwicktologist lauren 

mafficked@gawker.com a_OK okies 

cg@gawker.com CarolineG dratini 

 

which of course means that if you ever reused that email address, username, or password at another website like your bank or Amazon (who doesn't?) can steal your money and access all sorts of private information. 

 

How do I fix it? 

 

Here's a picture of the end result: http://lts.cr/UhX [3] 

 

make a spreadsheet in google docs. make sure it's private[4]. 

add a link to the document to your Chrome bookmarks bar, and configure chrome to sync your bookmarks across computers 

generate a bunch of strong passwords. Here's a generator. 

paste the passwords in a column 

next time you go to a website, add the username and password to the document 

if the password sucks, reset the password right now. copy an unused password from the document and paste it right into the website password field. 

use your browser to remember the password 

 

Save the spreadsheet to your local harddrive and print out a copy. If google stops working, we want no-hassle access to our banks and stuff. Careful with backups on other web services like dropbox, they can get hacked too. 

 

Caveats: Memorize a secure password for google, and change it regularly -- if your google account is compromised, so are all your credentials. (Remember, most websites have password reset by email, so if your google account is compromised you were screwed anyway!) Be aware, if you might be the target of government monitoring or a subpeona, this won't work for you, because google will just hand your information over to any government that asks. But you already knew that.
